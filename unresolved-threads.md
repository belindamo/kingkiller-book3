### 3. Kvothe's "Fall" and Current State

- Kvothe is known as "Kingkiller"â€”he killed a king

### 4. The Amyr
**What We Know:**
- An ancient order dedicated to "the greater good"
- The original Amyr were non-human, serving the empire before the Church created a human version
- Supposedly disbanded by the Church centuries ago
- Records of them are mysteriously disappearing from the Archives
- Their symbol is a bloody hand
- Lorren may be secretly one of them (or protecting them)

**What We Don't Know:**
- Do they still exist?
- Are they allied against the Chandrian or something else?
- What is their relationship to the Ciridae (judges who could kill without consequence)?
- Why are they erasing their own history?

**Resolution in Book 3:** The Amyr never disbandedâ€”they went underground. Lorren is revealed as a member. Their "greater good" involves containing something worse than the Chandrian, and Kvothe's meddling threatens to release it. The Amyr become antagonists who see Kvothe as a threat.

**What We Don't Know:**
- What lies behind the door(s)?

**Resolution in Book 3:** The Lackless door guards the prison of something from the Creation Warâ€”possibly Iax/Jax himself, who stole the moon. Kvothe's heritage gives him access. Opening the door is what causes the current catastrophe in the frame story.
