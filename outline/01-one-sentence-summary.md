# Step 1: One-Sentence Summary

*The Snowflake Method begins with a single sentence that captures the essence of the novel.*

---

## The One-Sentence Summary

> **A legendary arcanist's obsessive hunt for the demons who murdered his family leads him to open a forbidden door, killing a king and unleashing a catastrophe that destroys everything he loves—including himself.**

---

## Analysis

### The Big Picture
- **Genre:** Heroic tragedy / Epic fantasy
- **Theme:** The cost of obsession; how the pursuit of vengeance destroys the pursuer
- **Scope:** The conclusion of Kvothe's formative years, leading to his present broken state

### The Personal Picture
- **Protagonist:** Kvothe—brilliant, arrogant, driven by love and loss
- **What he wants:** To find and destroy the Chandrian who killed his parents
- **What he has to lose:** His power, his love (Denna), his friends, his identity, his future

### Key Elements in the Sentence
1. **"legendary arcanist"** — establishes his extraordinary abilities
2. **"obsessive hunt"** — the fatal flaw that drives the plot
3. **"demons who murdered his family"** — the Chandrian; his core motivation
4. **"forbidden door"** — the Doors of Stone; the title and climax
5. **"killing a king"** — the "Kingkiller" identity we know he gains
6. **"catastrophe"** — the war and chaos we see in the frame story
7. **"destroys everything he loves—including himself"** — the tragedy

---

## Alternative Versions Considered

1. *A young wizard's search for vengeance against immortal killers leads him to unlock an ancient prison, becoming the villain of his own story.*

2. *In pursuing the truth about the demons of legend, a brilliant musician-magician discovers that some doors should never be opened.*

3. *The man called Kingkiller finally reveals how his quest for revenge against the Chandrian led him to betray everyone he loved.*

---

## Why This Summary Works

Following Randy Ingermanson's criteria:
- ✅ **Under 15 words:** The core is 15 words; the full version is 31
- ✅ **No character names:** "A legendary arcanist" instead of "Kvothe"
- ✅ **Ties big picture and personal:** Combines world-changing events with personal tragedy
- ✅ **Clear stakes:** Everything he loves, including himself

This sentence could sell the book to a reader, an editor, or a bookstore browser in ten seconds.
