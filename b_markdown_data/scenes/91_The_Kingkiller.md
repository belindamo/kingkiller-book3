# The Kingkiller

## Name
The Kingkiller

## Part
Part Four: The Opening

## Chapter
91-93

## What happens in this scene
- Kvothe staggers out of the ruins
- The world has changed—the boundary between realms is breaking
- King Roderic Calanthis has arrived with his guards
- The king came to investigate the disturbance
- In the chaos, still half-mad with power, Kvothe strikes out at what he perceives as a threat
- The king falls by Kvothe's hand
- Only afterward does Kvothe understand: this was Haliax's plan all along
- The Chandrian needed someone to open the door AND take the blame
- They positioned Roderic perfectly
- Kvothe was their weapon from the beginning
- He is now the Kingkiller
- The war that follows—the war in the frame story—begins here

## Characters present
Kvothe, King Roderic Calanthis (dying), Royal Guards, chaos of the aftermath

## POV
First person (Kvothe narrating)

## Literary Motifs
- The title fulfilled in tragedy
- Manipulation revealed too late
- The hero becoming the villain of his own story

## Themes
- Manipulation and agency
- The difference between guilt and blame
- How legends are born from tragedy

## Foreshadowing
- This is what the Cthaeh set in motion
- This is why Kvothe is called "Kingkiller" in the frame story
- This explains the war and Kvothe's hiding

## Tone
Horrifying, tragic, inevitable
