# The Third Day

## Name
The Third Day (Frame Opening)

## Part
Prologue / Frame

## Chapter
1

## What happens in this scene
- The Waystone Inn on the third morning; the silence has changed
- Kvothe (as Kote) prepares to continue his story
- Bast watches anxiously; his plan to reawaken Kvothe through storytelling isn't working as expected
- Chronicler reviews his notes from the previous two days
- Kvothe warns them that today's story will be harder
- Outside, something is wrong—the scrael are gathering

## Characters present
Kvothe/Kote, Bast, Chronicler

## POV
Third person (frame) transitioning to First person (Kvothe's narration)

## Literary Motifs
- The silence of three parts (evolved from Books 1 & 2)
- Light and shadow imagery (the inn holding dim light "like a cupped hand holds water")
- The transformation between Kote and Kvothe as he remembers

## Themes
- Memory as identity
- The weight of story
- Waiting vs. living

## Foreshadowing
- "Today will be harder" — warns of the tragedy to come
- Scrael gathering — the external threat closing in
- Bast's anxiety — his plan may be backfiring

## Setting
The Waystone Inn, Newarre, present day (frame story)

## Tone
Quiet, heavy, anticipatory—the calm before the storm

## Key Lines
- "It was the third day, and the silence had changed."
- "I have to tell you about the things that ended. About doors that shouldn't be opened. About the king I killed, and why."
- "And about her?" / "Yes. About her."
