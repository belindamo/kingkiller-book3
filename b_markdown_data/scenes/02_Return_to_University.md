# Return to University

## Name
Return to University

## Part
Part One: The Return

## Chapter
2-4

## What happens in this scene
- Kvothe arrives at the University in late autumn
- The familiar buildings feel different—smaller, or perhaps he has grown
- He recognizes he has changed from a student seeking knowledge to "a weapon seeking a target"
- Reunion with Simmon and Wilem in the courtyard
- They share rumors about his adventures (Fae, bandits in the Eld)
- Wilem notices subtle changes in Kvothe—how he moves, how his eyes don't quite meet theirs
- Kvothe tells them he's found answers about his parents' killers
- He smiles, and it's not a kind smile

## Characters present
Kvothe, Simmon, Wilem

## POV
First person (Kvothe narrating)

## Literary Motifs
- Autumn/burning leaves as metaphor for transformation and coming destruction
- The University as a place that shaped him, now feeling inadequate
- The contrast between innocent students and what Kvothe has become

## Themes
- Homecoming as alienation
- The cost of knowledge
- Friendship and the burden of secrets

## Foreshadowing
- "a weapon seeking a target" — Kvothe's self-awareness of what he's become
- Wilem's perceptive notice of changes — his friends can see something is wrong
- The "not kind" smile — warning of the darker Kvothe emerging

## Setting
University, Imre, Four Corners (story)

## Tone
Bittersweet reunion undercut with tension

## Key Lines
- "I returned to the University in the late days of autumn, when the leaves were just beginning to burn."
- "What arrived at the University that afternoon was something else."
- "I'm going to find them. And when I do, I'm going to make them answer for what they've done."
